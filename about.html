About Me

I am a third-year undergraduate at Northwestern University studying computer science, with research interests in computing education and human-computer interaction. I am advised by Haoqi Zhang as part of the Design, Technology, and Research group under the Delta Lab. My research explores the concept of leveraging professional websites as worked examples of front-end development techniques, along with possible methods for integrating such examples into the end-user programming workflow.

I'm enthusiastic about teaching, and dedicated to making computer science educationally accessible for students who lack confidence in their abilities. I am currently a head teaching assistant for EECS 111: Fundamentals of Computer Programming I at Northwestern, as well as EECS 214: Data Structures, where I work to help develop effective assignments, assessments, and pedagogical practices for introductory computer science curriculum.

My work has received recognition from companies and organizations including Google, Palantir, Quip, Lime Connect, and The Alumnae of Northwestern University. Over the summer, I was a User Interface Engineering Intern at LinkedIn in Sunnyvale, CA, where I built an Ember.js addon for SVG data visualization.
